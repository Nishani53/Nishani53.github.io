Hello! I am **Naresh Khanal**, a researcher focused on the intersection of forest economics, policy, and regional modeling. 

With an academic foundation rooted in Nepal and the United States, I bring a global and multidisciplinary perspective to forest resource economics and sustainable forest management. I recently completed my MSc at Oregon State University, focusing on evaluating the economic feasibility of utilizing low-value biomass for high-value products like mass timber, particularly in fire-prone landscapes in Oregon’s Blue Mountains.

I hold dual master's degrees in forestry from Oregon State University and Michigan State University, where I led and contributed to projects on forest product markets, economic impact modelling (IMPLAN), and restoration prioritization. My work has involved collaboration with state agencies like the Michigan Department of Natural Resources and research presentations at global conferences, including IUFRO and SAF.

Previously, I contributed to large-scale conservation efforts in Nepal, including drafting national action plans for tiger and dolphin conservation. My fieldwork has taken me from the Himalayas to the forests of the Pacific Northwest, collecting ecological and economic data that inform both conservation and commercial forestry.

🔍 Key Tools & Skills: Forest economics, ArcGIS, R, Python, IMPLAN, Econometrics, Biomass Modelling, Restoration Optimization, Forest Operations Planning, and Optimization

🪵 Research Focus: Valuing non-market forest goods, forest supply chains, forest carbon policy, wood utilization innovation, wildfire risk reduction economics

If you’re passionate about forestry innovation, biomass utilization, or the intersection of ecology and economics, let’s connect. I’m always open to collaborations, insights, or conversations about the future of sustainable forest landscapes.

### Research Focus
My research combines economic modeling (IMPLAN) with spatial analysis to address critical challenges in:
* **Forest Resource Economics:** Valuing ecosystem services and optimizing timber supply chains.
* **Mass Timber & Bioeconomy:** Assessing the market reach and industrial impacts of low-value timber utilization.
* **Forest Policy:** Analyzing the socio-economic effects of wildfire reduction and regional forest treatments.
