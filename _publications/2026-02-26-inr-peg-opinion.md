In this column, I examine whether Nepal's long-standing currency peg to the Indian Rupee remains a force for stability or has become an economic burden. As Nepal approaches its graduation from Least Developed Country (LDC) status, the article discusses the trade-offs between monetary stability and export competitiveness, citing lessons from Bhutan, Hong Kong, and Argentina.
